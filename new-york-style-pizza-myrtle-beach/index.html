Real New York-style pizza is hard to fake. Most places on the Grand Strand don't come close. One does: Genova's Pizza & Pasta on Dick Pond Road. Locally owned, family recipes, made fresh every day.

The Breakdown

What Makes Real NY-Style Pizza

◊

Hand-Tossed Dough

Authentic NY pizza starts with dough made fresh daily and hand-tossed — not pressed or rolled. This creates the characteristic thin center with a slightly thicker, airy crust edge.

◊

The Fold Test

A real NY slice holds its shape when folded in half lengthwise. It's flexible but not soggy, crispy on the bottom but with a soft interior. That balance is everything.

◊

Sicilian & Grandma Style

Beyond the round pie, authentic NY pizza culture includes thick Sicilian squares baked in an oiled pan, and Grandma-style — a thinner, crispier variation of the Sicilian that's become iconic.

◊

Simple, Quality Ingredients

NY pizza isn't about fancy toppings — it's about quality basics. Fresh dough, real mozzarella, house-made sauce. The simplicity is the point.

◊

By the Slice Culture

In New York, pizza is sold by the slice, reheated on the deck of the oven. It's fast, casual, and perfect. Genova's carries that same slice culture to Myrtle Beach.

◊

Family Recipes

The best NY pizza comes from recipes passed down through families, not corporate formulas. Genova's is locally owned with family recipes at the core of everything they make.
